# Long Live The King Rules Notes

Source: `Long_Live_the_King_Spanish_Rules_Version_1.0.pdf`, supplied June 18, 2026.

Use the spreadsheet as the source of truth for detailed character sheets, role numbers, exact abilities, and victory conditions. Use the PDF as the source of truth for the general rules structure.

## Integrated Structure

- The game runs for 7 turns.
- Each turn has 3 phases: Audience, Diplomacy, Council.
- Audience is an administrative phase resolution led by the King or chamberlain. It can be run directly through the app, or played orally/physically at the table and then recorded in the app.
- Diplomacy is the free negotiation phase.
- Council is the formal petition phase.
- Votes in Council are weighted by Favor while the King presides.
- If the King is sick, the heir or regent may rule directly; without an heir, Council becomes democratic.
- Status controls petition order and voting order, from low Status upward, with high Status interruption rights.
- Intrigue cards are freely tradable and start at 2 per main courtier.
- Status cards are distributed from the role's current `status` resource.
- Audience income and Intrigue draws are handled through the `hold-audience` action, with odd/even turn draw counts, Queen fixed draws, Archbishop bonus support, and low-Status income reduction.
- Health uses 10 cards, with 6 lined up at setup; no health card is normally revealed on turn 1.
- Mobilization and Rebellion are public 5-card tracks.
- The last Council cannot be missed by the King.
